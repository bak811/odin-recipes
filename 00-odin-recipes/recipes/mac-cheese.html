Macaroni and cheese

Description

Mac and cheese, short for macaroni and cheese, is a comforting and indulgent dish that features small pasta shapes, typically elbow macaroni, coated in a creamy cheese sauce. The dish often starts with a rich béchamel sauce made from butter, flour, and milk, which is then mixed with a generous amount of cheese—common choices include cheddar, mozzarella, or a blend for extra flavor.

The texture is creamy and smooth, with a delightful contrast between the tender pasta and the gooey cheese. Many recipes also include a crispy breadcrumb topping, adding a satisfying crunch. Whether baked in the oven or prepared on the stovetop, mac and cheese is a beloved comfort food that can be enjoyed as a main dish or a side, often evoking feelings of nostalgia and warmth.

Ingredients

  • 8 oz elbow macaroni
  • 2 cups shredded sharp cheddar cheese
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese (optional for creaminess)
  • 3 tablespoons butter
  • 3 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups milk (whole milk is best)
  • 1 teaspoon mustard powder (optional for flavor)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika or cayenne pepper (optional for a bit of heat)
  • 1 cup breadcrumbs (for topping, optional)

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Pasta:
    • Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the macaroni and cook according to package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  2. Make the Cheese Sauce:
    • In a large saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Once melted, whisk in the flour and cook for about 1-2 minutes until it’s slightly golden.
    • Gradually whisk in the milk, stirring constantly to avoid lumps. Cook until the mixture thickens and comes to a simmer, about 5-7 minutes.
  3. Combine:
    • the cooked macaroni to the cheese sauce and stir until the pasta is well coated.
  4. Optional Baking:
    • If you want a crispy topping, preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C)
    • Transfer the mac and cheese to a baking dish. In a small bowl, mix breadcrumbs with a little melted butter, then sprinkle over the top.
    • Bake for 20-25 minutes or until the top is golden and bubbly.
  5. Serve:
    • Let it cool for a few minutes before serving. Enjoy your creamy, cheesy mac and cheese!
