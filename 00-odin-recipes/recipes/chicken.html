Orange Chicken

Description

Orange chicken is a popular dish in Chinese-American cuisine, featuring bite-sized pieces of chicken that are lightly battered and fried until crispy.

Tossed in a vibrant, tangy sauce made from fresh orange juice, soy sauce, garlic, and ginger, it delivers a delightful balance of sweetness and zest. Often garnished with green onions and served over a bed of fluffy rice, orange chicken is both comforting and indulgent, making it a favorite among many.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb boneless, skinless chicken (thighs or breasts), cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 cup orange juice
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup cornstarch
  • Optional: garlic or ginger for extra flavor, and green onions for garnish

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Chicken:
    • In a bowl, toss the chicken pieces with cornstarch until they are evenly coated. This will help create a crispy texture when cooked.
  2. Make the Sauce:
    • In a separate bowl, mix the orange juice and soy sauce. If using garlic or ginger, you can add minced garlic or grated ginger to the sauce at this stage.
  3. Cook the Chicken:
    • Heat a bit of oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add the coated chicken pieces and cook until they are golden brown and cooked through, about 5-7 minutes. Remove the chicken from the skillet and set aside.
  4. Combine and Simmer:
    • In the same skillet, add the orange juice and soy sauce mixture. Bring it to a simmer, allowing it to thicken slightly (about 3-5 minutes). If you want a thicker sauce, you can mix a little cornstarch with water and add it to the sauce.
  5. Add the Chicken:
    • Return the cooked chicken to the skillet, tossing it in the sauce until it's well coated. Let it cook for an additional minute or two.
  6. Serve:
    • Garnish with sliced green onions if desired. Serve the orange chicken over rice or with vegetables.
